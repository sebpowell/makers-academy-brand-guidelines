Photography

Photography is used throughout the branded materials, usually as a backdrop over which the logo and supporting copy are overlayed.

We like to use glossy, high resolution imagery. This will mostly be ‘action shots’ of our students &or staff- at a desk pair programming, doing a standup in a group, taking part in a breakout session, using a Kanban board, sketching ideas on a whiteboard etc.

We mix this up with ‘lifestyle’ images that promote the things we think matter - health and fitness (eg shots of the bikes we encourage people to ride or students on a group-climb), mental well-being (shots of yoga and meditation), or ‘fun’ shots of students & staff chatting, eating, generally going about their lives, and always happy.

Background Photos

Background photos have the following treatment applied to them:

  1. Convert the image to greyscale (in Photoshop: Image > Adjustments > Desaturate)
  2. Add a Maker's Navy color overlay on top, with layer opacity set to around 60%
  3. You may need to adjust the contrast to ensure the image details come through the overlay.

Brand Story

We understand that Makers Academy is expensive - we’d call it ‘reassuringly expensive’. We know that for the vast majority of our students, the cost of the course is a significant part of their life savings, or may even have to be covered with a loan - which they’ll need a job to pay back.

The Makers Academy brand promise is this - if we offer you a place on the course, it’s because we genuinely believe that we can turn you into a professional developer over the next 3-6 months.

We’ve built a strong brand built on trust, derived from delivering on our promise - that Makers Academy is the best vocational route into the world of software development.

If we take your money, we’re betting our brand on you. If you do your bit, and work your socks off, we’ll do ours, and support you until you’re working as a developer.

Differentiators

We’re not a school - we’re the best developer factory in the world.

Where our competitors focus on being the best ‘educational establishment’, we focus on being the best way to get a job as a junior developer. We charge our hiring partners, so our goals, and our customers goals, are aligned. We always use best practise. We never cut corners.

Makers Academy is more than just a ‘school’ - it’s a mindset. It’s a way of life.

Tone of Voice

The Makers Academy brand is knowledgeable but not condescending, confident not arrogant, humble, friendly, direct and playful.

The Makers Academy brand tells it like it is. It uses simple language, but it’s quite partial to a cheeky turn-of-phrase. Makers Academy is honest and trustworthy, and if it says it’ll do something, you can bet your bottom dollar it’ll follow through.

The tech world is complicated enough - we’ll do our best to speak plainly and explain concepts in a way that anyone could understand. We won’t mince our words or use jargon to make ourselves sound clever. The tone of Makers Academy’s writing is light and conversational. We use contractions - “it is” would always be “it’s” when Makers Academy’s talking.

Our language always reflects a core part of the Makers Academy ethos, placing ‘fun’ at the heart of what we do.
